1. Krill

This is a row of crustaceans whose individuals do not exceed 2 cm in length. Some are herbivores and feed on phytoplankton by filtration from seawater, others are carnivores, eating larvae and eggs, but most eat everything.



2. Blue Whale

This is the largest animal that exists, reaching up to 30 m in length and weighing over 100 tons, hunted to use its oil, liver and meat. This cetacean inhabits all the oceans of the planet and feeds by filtering water through its bales to capture tons of krill.



3. Dolphins

This is another marine cetacean, of which several marine and freshwater species are known. They are very intelligent animals that use sound to locate submerged objects in their underwater movements. There are 34 known species of ocean dolphins that reach between 2 and 8 meters in length, as well as 7 species of river dolphins with a length of 1.6 to 2 meters.




4. Manatee

Manatee - Manatee, also known as "sea cow", is the name given to four species of herbivorous mermaid mammals that inhabit sea waters and rivers in America and Africa. They can reach up to 6 meters in length and weigh 500 kilograms, and have a flattened spatula-shaped tail fin.



5. Clown Fish

With this name are grouped about 29 species of omnivorous fish of the family Amphiprioninae, which live among the tentacles of anemones. Due to the mucous layer that covers its scales, it does not receive anemone bites, maintaining a symbiotic relationship. Therefore, both species benefit, the clownfish gains security while cleaning and protecting the anemone from predators.



6. White Shark

It is one of the largest marine predators, reaching 5 to 7 meters in length. Despite its name, its color is not white, except on the abdomen, and its upper part is gray-blue. This shark inhabits the continental shelf of almost all oceans except the coldest (Arctic and Antarctic) and feeds on cetaceans, seals, sea turtles, tuna and other large prey.



7. Octopus

Octopuses are called species of octopuses that are soft bodies of aquatic animals and equipped with 8 tentacles with suction cups. At the bottom, in the center of the tentacle ring, they have a mouth or beak and a back siphon through which they breathe. This siphon also discharges a stream of water, which allows it to move under drive. They are animals with great intelligence and very good eyesight.



8. Green Turtle

It is one of the 7 species of turtles that inhabit the tropical and subtropical seas, and its common name comes from the green color of its fat. The adults of these aquatic animals feed on seagrass and lay their eggs buried on sandy beaches.




9. Corals

Coral reefs, which develop in the shallow, warm waters of tropical and subtropical seas, are made up of coral polyps. These are organisms that form colonies of thousands of individuals and are covered with calcareous growths that form a reef. The different colors that corals show are due to algae that grow in symbiosis with them, called zooxanthellae.



10. Piranha

This name applies to a number of carnivorous freshwater fish that inhabit the rivers of Central and South America. They are characterized by attacking their prey in groups in a very aggressive way. Piranhas have a flat and compact body with long and sharp teeth, as well as fast and excited swimming when attacked.
