1. Pelicans Protected Area

Pelicanite Protected Area - Pelicanite Protected Area is a former buffer zone of the Srebarna Maintained Reserve. Construction is prohibited on its territory, except for treatment and service facilities related to maintaining or improving the ecological condition of the reserve. Coming from the name, pelicans can most often be found there.





2. Kolokita Protected Area

Kolokita Protected Site - The Kolokita Protected Site was declared on June 16, 1970 by the Ministry of Forestry and Forest Industry. The protected area covers part of the Budjaka Peninsula, and the core of the peninsula is outside it. The announcement order does not allow construction, as it is specified that the construction and improvement of the protected area will be allowed with the development of a comprehensive development project for this part of the Southern Black Sea coast.


3. Poda Protected Area

Poda Protected Area - In addition to the exceptional diversity of birds, it is home to the only mixed colony of white-tailed deer, shiny ibis, small cormorant, night, red, maned, small white and gray heron on our Black Sea coast. The international significance of Poda for birds defines it as an Ornithologically Important Site, a Natura 2000 Area and a Ramsar Site.




4. Durankulak Lake Protected Area

Durankulak Lake Protected Area - The lake is among the best preserved coastal wetlands in Bulgaria. Of international importance is the conservation of over 260 species of endemic, rare and protected from extinction plants and animals that occur in the area. 12 species of mammals, 23 species of fish, 7 species of amphibians and 5 species of reptiles have been registered in Lake Durankulak. The lake is the only proven locality in Bulgaria for wild carp storage.






5. Shabla Lake Protected Area

Shabla Lake Protected Area - 247 species of birds have been identified on the territory of the area, some of which are included in the Red Book of Bulgaria. 137 species of birds are of European importance. World-endangered species such as the corncrake and the white-eyed woodpecker nest in the protected area of ​​Shabla Lake. During the autumn-winter season can be seen globally endangered species such as the thorny-tailed grebe, Dalmatian pelican, small cormorant. In winter, 80-90% of the world's red-breasted goose finds shelter in Shabla Lake. There are 23 more species of fish in Shabla Lake, seven of which are included in the Red Book of Bulgaria.


6. Kalimok-Brushlen Protected Area

Kalimok-Brushlen Protected Site - The aim of the site is to preserve the diversity of ecosystems and landscapes typical of the region: natural swamps, riparian and wetland habitats. Kalimok-Brushlen has 109 species of algae, 10 species of mosses, 16 species of fungi, over 300 higher plants, over 240 species of birds, of which over 130 are nesting.




7. Zlato Pole Protected Area

Zlato Pole Protected Area - The presence of a newly formed mixed heron colony of gray heron, little egret, maned heron, night heron determines its importance to the area on a national scale. There is a stable nesting population of the species small water bull and small diver, periodic nesting has been established on the globally endangered species of white-eyed diver. The Syrian garlic population in the region is one of the most stable in the country, with the species using the reservoirs in the protected area for breeding.
