Protected Animals

The International Union for Conservation of Nature (IUCN) establishes global red lists. These lists contain the names of species that have some degree of threat of extinction, which gives them a category according to the level of risk. Here are some of the most endangered animals on these lists:

1. Red Tone

It is a fish that can exceed 3 meters in length and weigh 400 kilograms and inhabits the Atlantic Ocean and the Mediterranean Sea. This species is endangered by overfishing worldwide and is considered one of the most endangered species today.





2. Marina from Vakita or Kochito

This species is a cetacean, a small guinea pig that lives in the Gulf of California and of which only about 15 individuals remain. According to the IUCN, the wakita guinea pig is a critically endangered species.




3. The Mediterranean Monk Seal

This marine cetacean inhabits the shores of the Mediterranean Sea and the Atlantic coast of North Africa and Portugal. It is estimated that only about 630 specimens remain, which is why IUCN lists it as an endangered species.




4. The Sea Otter

It is a carnivorous marine mammal that lives in the North Pacific, from Japan and Baja California (Mexico). He has legs and can go ashore, but spends most of his life in the water. An population of just over 100,000 sea otters has been estimated.




5. The giant Otter of the Amazon

It is a carnivorous freshwater mammal that lives in the rivers of the Amazon Basin and faces a great threat of extinction, as only about 5,000 specimens of its genus remain.
